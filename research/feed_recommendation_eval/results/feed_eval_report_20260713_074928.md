## C. Literature-Benchmarked Simulation

Simulated a 180-day tilapia grow-out cycle using the production feeding formula against a representative growth curve and seasonal Bangladesh pond temperature profile (see module docstring for the exact assumptions — these are illustrative, not measured, data).

- Total simulated feed used: **436.6 kg**
- Total weight gain: **247.0 kg** (1000 stocked → 900 surviving)
- **Implied FCR: 1.77**

This falls within the general literature FCR range (1.2–2.0), and within the typical range reported in Bangladesh tilapia pond studies (1.7–1.97), suggesting the feeding formula's calibration is biologically plausible.

## Methodology note — size-based feeding adjustment

An earlier version of this formula scaled feed rate by **water temperature only**. A first simulation run (documented in this project's evaluation history) showed this over-fed fish once they grew past ~150g, producing an implied FCR (~2.6) well outside the literature range. Aquaculture feeding-rate guidance indicates feeding rate should decrease sharply as fish grow (e.g. ~15-20% BW/day for fry vs. ~1-3% BW/day near harvest size), independent of temperature. A `_size_factor()` multiplier (see `feed_calculator.py`) was added to address this — its effect on the simulated FCR is shown in Section C above. The multiplier bands are an engineering approximation calibrated against general feeding-rate guidance rather than a single peer-reviewed table specific to this species/system; refining them further with real farm data remains a reasonable direction for future work.

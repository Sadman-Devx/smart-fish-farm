## C. Literature-Benchmarked Simulation

Simulated a 180-day tilapia grow-out cycle using the production feeding formula against a representative growth curve and seasonal Bangladesh pond temperature profile (see module docstring for the exact assumptions — these are illustrative, not measured, data).

- Total simulated feed used: **644.4 kg**
- Total weight gain: **247.0 kg** (1000 stocked → 900 surviving)
- **Implied FCR: 2.61**

This falls outside the general literature FCR range (1.2–2.0), and outside the typical range reported in Bangladesh tilapia pond studies (1.7–1.97), suggesting the feeding formula's calibration is worth revisiting — consider adjusting FeedingProfile rates.

## Known limitation (for paper's discussion/limitations section)

The current feeding formula scales feed rate by **water temperature only** (via `FeedingProfile`). Aquaculture literature (e.g. FAO on-farm feed management guidance) indicates feeding rate should also decrease as a **percentage of body weight as fish grow larger** (e.g. ~15–20% BW/day for fry vs. ~1–3% BW/day near harvest size), independent of temperature. This system does not currently apply a size/age-based adjustment — a reasonable direction for future work.

## Sources

- FAO. *On-farm feeding and feed management in aquaculture* — temperature and body-mass jointly determine appropriate feeding rate.
- Rahman & Islam (2021), *Sinking Feed Efficiency in Growth Performance, FCR and Yield of Tilapia*, Bangladesh Agricultural University — FCR 1.70–1.97 in Bangladesh pond/tank tilapia trials.
- General aquaculture industry guidance: ideal FCR range 1.2–2.0 (lower is better feed efficiency).
